    How it works

    For every remaining ship length, the AI enumerates every horizontal and vertical placement still consistent with the board (no placement may cross a confirmed miss or a cell already resolved as part of a sunk ship) and tallies, per cell, how many valid placements cover it. Cells covered by more possible placements are more likely to actually hold a ship, so the AI fires at the highest-density unknown cell. Once a hit is still unresolved, the AI narrows to placements that cover that hit ("target mode"), which naturally finishes off a found ship before hunting a new one — without any hardcoded direction logic.

    The enemy fleet you attack is placed by the same style of algorithm in reverse: it plays many simulated games against the Probability AI for a batch of random legal layouts and keeps whichever one survives the longest on average — the "Auto-Place (Smart)" button uses the same search for your own fleet.

    Static, client-side JS — no backend. See ai.js for the AI implementation.
